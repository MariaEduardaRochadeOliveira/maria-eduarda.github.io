About me

Title

Hey! I am Maria Eduarda Rocha de Oliveira, born in Ribeirão Preto, Brazil. At the age of 13 I started my journey in social actions by volunteering at the church to help low-income people in my community ,and this was just the beginning of my story ! In my elementary school I participated in a math competition that awakened my passion for education, where I started, even at such a young age, to be a math mentor helping my colleagues in school. As a grown woman, I discovered that I could be the change I wanted to see in my society through social entrepreneurship. Since then I have been acting as a social leader founding my own projects focused on education and youth leadership. Nowadays I have international recognition, support from more than 11 companies and institutions such as the US Embassy in my social projects. Currently, I aspire to enhance the educational scenario of Brazil to support the rise of young leaders.

Extracurriculars!

OBMLibras

Title

During my gap year, I co-founded the social project OBMLibras, the first mathematics olympiad in sign language of Brazil. In partnership with more than 10 companies and the US Embassy, we offer awards such as medals, book kits, summer programs at universities in Brazil, and scholarships for academic courses in sign language. With international recognition, in two years we have already impacted more than 3 thousand deaf students from 16 Brazilian states and received 26 thousand brazilian reais of investment. Furthermore, we are also developing a Physics and Astronomy Olympiad, once again, being the first in Brazil to operate in this segment for people with disabilities.

Experience of a student

Students participating in the olympiad declared that they loved an opportunity where they could use their own language. Vitor won second place in the elementary school category, he mentioned that on the day of the olympiad he studied a lot to be able to take the test in the afternoon. The OBMLibras inspired him to study Math in a deep way . While he was waiting for the results he was nervous ,not feeling really confident, but when he found out that he won the second place he started to cry because he was extremely happy.

ProLíder Program

Title

The ProLíder is a business, economic, and political science program, which sponsored me with monthly trips to São Paulo State for 8 months. It’s Brazil’s most selective fellowship program with over 14 thousand applicants and an 0.48% acceptance rate. Within the program, I attended classes at IBMEC College, one of the most renewed economic & business-centered colleges in Brazil. I was required to read articles, analyze research, and turn in college-level coursework featuring Brazil’s business, economics, and political scenarios. In parallel, it was an opportunity to network with Brazil's finest leaders, such as the president of the Central Bank and board members of the Brazilian Government. Moreover, In March 2024, I went on a one month exchange to Boston with all expenses covered. This was my third fully funded exchange to the United States. In Boston, I took college-level classes with professors from MIT and Harvard, focusing on social entrepreneurship and global issues.

Latin American Leadership Academy (LALA)

Title

LALA is a non-profit institution that aims to develop a new generation of diverse, entrepreneurial, and ethical leaders who will serve the Latin American region. I was a Mentee of the LALA program the University Placement (UP), a 12-month virtual program that offers free support for academically brilliant students engaged in social impact activities to apply for degree programs at US universities and abroad. Because of my standout performance within the NGO, I was invited to represent the institution at official institutional dinners, sharing my leadership story with philanthropic investors. As LALeader, I am part of a lifelong community of changemakers working to address the region’s most pressing challenges.

Hortitude

Title

During my high school, I founded the “Hortitude” project, a community garden that aims to offer healthy food to public school students. To improve local sustainability, the project is made of objects that would be thrown away, like car tires. “Hortitude” is the combination of the words garden plus attitude in portuguese. I strongly believe that only through actions I can be the change I hope to see in my community. The project has already impacted more than 2.000 low income students and 150 kg of food were donated. Hortitude was the first vegetable garden created at my school, Eugênia Vilhena de Morais, by this initiative we became a reference of sustainable projects in the city.

Title

During my gap year, I was a gold medalist in the Prudential Young Visionaries Awards. My project OBMLibras stood out among submissions from all over Brazil, being awarded as the best social project. Youth Ambassadors

Title

In 2022 I was one of 50 students selected from 10.000 applications to represent Brazil in the Youth Ambassadors exchange. The Youth Ambassadors Program is an official initiative of the US Department of State and, in Brazil, is coordinated by the US Embassy and Consulates America. The program targets students from Brazil who are in public high school and who stand out in their respective communities for their positive attitude, excellent academic performance, knowledge of the English language, leadership skills and entrepreneurial spirit. After two years applying, in 2022 I went to Washington DC to learn about international relations and social actions for global problems. In Chicago I visited NGOs, did volunteer work and was able to meet leaders from different parts of the world. In the program I developed my knowledge about Brazil, by having contact with young people from several states and I developed a deep self knowledge of how I can use my skills to improve my country.

Mapa Educação

Title

Mapa Educação is a non-governmental organization that believes that young people should be the protagonist in changing the country. Our mission is that all Brazilians have access to quality education and we work to make education a priority on the national political agenda. During the one-year mentorship “Journey of the leader”, I had weekly masterclasses with ministers of justice, Harvard students and members of the Brazilian government. At the end of the initiative, I developed a capstone project with other ambassadors aiming to tackle school evasion. With my active participation, I received the title of Leader Mapa Educação, an honor given only to outstanding ambassadors. I currently work at Mapa to improve Brazilian public education for students with disabilities.

Ashoka Young Changemakers

Title

Ashoka has been a pioneer in the field of social entrepreneurship, with the objective of boosting social transformations through recognition and support for innovative social entrepreneurs. Ashoka Young Changemakers are a carefully selected network of young people who have found their power to create change for the good of all around them. They engage their peers and societies in realizing a world where everyone is a changemaker. After months of a rigorous selection process with essays, national and international interviews, I was one of the 21 Brazilians selected to join the global network of Young Changemakers Ashoka. Nowadays, I am a activist in favor of the movement a “A World of People who Transform”, inspiring other young people to be changemakers in their communities.
